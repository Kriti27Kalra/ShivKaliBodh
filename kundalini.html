Kundalini Yoga

Awakening the divine power within

What is Kundalini?

Kundalini is the divine feminine energy believed to reside at the base of the spine. In the ShivKaliBodh path, this energy is none other than Maa Kali in her most sacred form—Maa Kundalini. When awakened through spiritual practices and divine grace, she rises through the chakras, purifying and empowering our being.

The ultimate goal of Kundalini Yoga is union with the all-pervading divine consciousness—leading to liberation, transformation, and eternal peace.

The Seven Chakras

1. Muladhara (Root Chakra)

Located at the base of the spine, it governs survival, grounding, and physical identity.

2. Swadhisthana (Sacral Chakra)

Located below the navel, it controls creativity, sensuality, and emotions.

3. Manipura (Solar Plexus Chakra)

Located above the navel, it fuels confidence, willpower, and personal power.

4. Anahata (Heart Chakra)

Located at the heart center, it radiates love, compassion, and forgiveness.

5. Vishuddha (Throat Chakra)

Located at the throat, it governs communication, truth, and self-expression.

6. Ajna (Third Eye Chakra)

Located between the eyebrows, it represents intuition, insight, and clarity.

7. Sahasrara (Crown Chakra)

Located at the top of the head, it connects to divine consciousness and universal energy.

Benefits of Kundalini Awakening

Spiritual Growth

Rapid evolution of consciousness and connection with divine energy.

Emotional Balance

Release of negative emotions and restoration of inner peace and clarity.

Physical Healing

Improved health, vitality, and energy through chakra purification.

The Journey Through Chakras

As Maa Kundalini ascends through the Sushumna channel, she activates each chakra—Muladhara, Swadhisthana, Manipura, Anahata, Vishuddha, Ajna, and finally Sahasrara—bringing specific spiritual powers and realizations. This journey leads to a state of blissful Samadhi where the seeker lives in continuous union with God.
